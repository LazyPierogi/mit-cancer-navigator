# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in this project a respectful, constructive, and safety-aware experience for everyone.

This repository sits at the intersection of software engineering, biomedical evidence review, and responsible AI. That means high standards for both collaboration and honesty. We want a community that is technically rigorous, respectful in disagreement, and careful about safety claims.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment include:

- showing empathy and respect toward other contributors
- giving actionable, technically grounded feedback
- being honest about limitations, uncertainty, and tradeoffs
- respecting different backgrounds, skill levels, and perspectives
- discussing clinical or AI-related topics without overstating certainty
- prioritizing auditability, provenance, and safety over hype

## Unacceptable behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, intimidation, or abusive language
- personal attacks, trolling, or inflammatory behavior
- publishing private, confidential, or identifying information without permission
- knowingly introducing secrets, unsafe data, or deceptive claims into the project
- misrepresenting experimental results, benchmark outcomes, or model behavior
- presenting the project as a licensed clinical system when it is not
- using the project space to promote harmful, deceptive, or irresponsible AI claims

## Responsible AI expectations

Because this project touches health-related workflows, contributors are expected to:

- avoid claiming clinical authority the system does not have
- make uncertainty visible rather than smoothing it over
- preserve inspectability and provenance where possible
- avoid contributions that encourage hidden prompt behavior as a substitute for explicit logic
- treat assistive LLM features as optional support layers, not as decision authority

## Enforcement responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct and may take appropriate action in response to unacceptable behavior.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within project spaces and in public interactions reasonably connected to the project, including issues, pull requests, discussions, and review conversations.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please contact the project maintainers through GitHub. Please include as much context as you can so the situation can be reviewed fairly and responsibly.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers may take actions including:

- requesting clarification or a behavior change
- removing content
- limiting participation in discussions
- closing or locking threads
- temporarily or permanently restricting participation
